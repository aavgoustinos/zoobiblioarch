About the Project

What is ZooBi(bli)oArch?

ZooBi(bli)oArch is a bibliographic database for animal bioarchaeological studies in the Eastern Mediterranean and Middle East (EMME), chronologically covering skeletal assemblages from prehistory to early modern times. This database was based on the Bi(bli)oArch initiative (https://www.biblioarch.com/) and emerged from the fact that many studies on faunal remains in the region have been published in national archaeology journals, excavation monographs, ‘grey’ reports, or as graduate theses, making them difficult to find, especially by international scholars. A further complicating factor is that many relevant studies have been published in the national languages of the region, hence they do not appear during any basic online search in English. ZooBi(bli)oArch aims at overcoming these limitations and promoting bioarchaeological research in the EMME by making zooarchaeological papers and reports more easily discoverable.
